Security Recommendation: Portland's Resistance

The Risks A Solution Implementation
The Risks

As a social justice group whose mission is to be a non-violent movement organizer, the safety and privacy of members Portland's Resistance and activists with whom you organize should be a priority to group leadership. The group works against established policy that is racist and oppressive and the establishment would work to hinder progress to create equity.

Since Portland's Resistance is specifically working to fight white nationalism and other causes of oppression in Oregon, the threats you face come from multiple directions. You could face outside forces such as white nationalist groups or the government intercepting your communications. This could lead to planned protests being met with violence, sabotage or other opposition. It could also lead to a man-in-the-middle attack that causes deception and miscommunication between group members. The risk is not limited to information about organizing, but includes personal data of members and activists.

A Solution

In order to prevent or mitigate possible attempts at surveillance and disruption, we recommend that Portland's Resistance utilize secure communication methods to continue organizing and planning. There is no reason to think that in today's political climate and threats of culture war that either the government or other groups wouldn't attempt to sabotage and threaten the work of Portland's Resistance. To stay a step ahead of such attempts would increase the safety of members and activists, as well as accelerate progress of the group's mission.

It has been documented that government entities like the FBI and CIA have used man-in-the-middle attacks to disrupt and hinder the work of social justice movements in the past.

A communication tool such as Signal is open source, meaning that the underlying code and functionality of the software is available for anyone in the world to see, examine, critique and offer fixes, which contributes to the integrity of the tool.

A secure communication method, one like Keybase, Signal or Whatsapp, uses end-to-end encryption to minimize the threat of other parties intercepting and being able to decrypt potentially sensitive communications. The only parties who can decrypt such messages are the ones who have the decrpytion keys, the people at the endpoint of communication. Even if messages are intercepted, they are basically useless to the party who has intercepted them.

Implementation

Portland's Resistance might choose to use an end-to-end encrptyted messaging system such as Signal that would minimize the risk of several modes of oppression being used against them. Using an encrypted messaging system would ensure that only the intended recipients can read the messages. Such a system would make it difficult for authorities to monitor and gather evidence on the group's activities, and if access was gained, the messages would still have to be deciphered, which, with today's encryption technology and the use of hash functions, is essentially infeasible.

Members would be able to discuss sensitive matters without leaving a trace on traditional platforms. Only trusted individuals within Portland's Resistance would have access to information and conversations.

While the is significant and strong protection, the risk is not entirely eliminated. Necessary precautions should still be taken to ensure protection and security.

To use Signal, the application would need to be downloaded to an iOS or Android device. Each member would create a group on signal with a suitable name and add each member's phone number to the group. When all members are added, you can communicate with encrypted messages, videos and voice calls. To verify security, group members can compare security codes within the Signal application.
